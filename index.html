NAVIGATING THE ROLLERCOASTER OF

CULTURESHOCK

After investing significant time in another culture, possibly learning a new language, you've had life-altering experiences that can challenge your sense of self. While you've evolved, your home world may have remained largely unchanged.

This journey of self-discovery, akin to a rollercoaster, encompasses culture shock, with its ups and downs. At its core, the W-Curve model serves as a powerful tool for navigating these fluctuations in cultural adaptation.

SCROLL TO BEGIN

Honeymoon Stage

You are starting a new adventure! Everything is new and exciting! The honeymoon phase is full of excitement, curiosity, trying new things, and stimulation. It is when you first arrive at a new place and are figuring out how everything works.

“I loved meeting my roommate and reconnecting with all my friends I met at orientation. I was pumped to live on my own and make decisions solely for myself.”

Culture Shock Stage

Shortly after arriving in a foreign culture, you may feel overwhelmed, frustrated, and disoriented. It's when the unfamiliar hits you suddenly, leaving you isolated and questioning your sense of belonging.

“Starting a new educational environment was overwhelming. I felt disconnected and left out at times. I questioned my capabilities and where I belonged, and I constantly compared my experiences to those of others.”

Recovery Stage

You are starting a new adventure! Everything is new and exciting! The honeymoon phase is full of excitement, curiosity, trying new things, and stimulation. It is when you first arrive at a new place and are figuring out how everything works.

“I loved meeting my roommate and reconnecting with all my friends I met at orientation. I was pumped to live on my own and make decisions solely for myself."

Adjustment Stage

Over time, you understand the local dynamics, establish routines, and become more comfortable while still questioning your previous ways. You embrace differences and feel a sense of belonging.

“Right now in college, I'm totally in this stage. I've totally adjusted to rooming with others, made some tight friends, and got the hang of my classes, nailing them.”

Honeymoon Stage at Home

After spending an extended period in a foreign culture, returning to your home country and culture can trigger a mix of emotions. Whether it's your first time or a repeat occurrence, the reunion with family, old friends, and even pets can fill you with happiness. You find comfort in being back in your familiar surroundings.

“Returning home after a year abroad was a heartwarming reunion with my family and friends. Everything felt familiar, but I also noticed I had changed a bit. It was a mix of happiness and confusion as I tried to find my place between my home and abroad life.”

Reverse Culture Shock

Returning to your home country after spending time abroad can be a bewildering experience. Everything you once knew so well may suddenly seem unfamiliar. It's like seeing your hometown through a different lens, noticing subtleties you never paid attention to before.

"Returning to my home country after time abroad brought reverse culture shock. Everything felt different, even though it was my home. It's a reminder that culture shock continues to be part of the journey."
